# Hello Blog!

When I was in elementary school math came very easy to me. While other kids struggle, exams were like a breeze for me. It was very natural from the beginning to choose a career; I wanted to be a mathematician. It was simple, it was the path of least effort.

Like many other kids, I also liked drawing. What I never liked was my own drawings. I simply felt wasn’t talented so I stopped drawing at all. As we grew, kids who never stopped doing it got really good, even the ones who didn’t seem talented back then. They never got discouraged, they kept pushing.

On the other hand, I started having trouble with high school calculus. I never really put an effort into it. As concepts started getting harder, I got the feeling that I was not in Kansas anymore.

It took me a few years to get back on track, but the important lesson that I learned is that you can’t accomplish great things without effort. Struggling is what makes you master something, failing is what makes you stronger, trying and failing, rinse and repeat. Failing is the only way to success.

Talent is a dangerous word. Of course, talent exists, and it is evident that every one of us is naturally good at some things and naturally bad at some others. But talent becomes meaningless without work, it is like a rough diamond just taken out from the mine, it must be shaped, it must be polished. Failure is our best ally. Comfort our worst enemy.

It has been a while since I wanted to exercise the writing muscle. Communication is really hard and very important. Everyone has a different configuration in their minds, a different background. I want to teach myself to be able to reach people beyond my comfort zone. I hope to improve with time, but I must start somewhere, there are no shortcuts. So this is my own particular “Hello, World!”.

Do or do not, never stop trying.
