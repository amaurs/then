# Stereo

An ongoing ritual. Each year, on his birthday, the artist takes a stereoscopic photograph — a practice that began in 2016 in the Necropolis of Colón in Havana and continued the following year in the crater of an extinct volcano east of Mexico City. The tradition has persisted since, accumulating a growing archive of dimensional self-portraits tied to place, time, and the passage of another year.

The stereo format is not incidental. The artist's fascination with anaglyphs and stereoscopy — rooted in a childhood encounter with 3D imagery in the pre-internet nineties — became the gateway to photography itself. The decision to work in stereo is both autobiographical and formal: a way of encoding depth, of insisting that a single viewpoint is never enough. Each birthday image is a proof of presence, and the series, taken together, becomes a meditation on the accumulation of years as a spatial phenomenon.
