# Loom

The notation for weaving on a loom maps directly onto matrix multiplication — a correspondence that is mathematical in nature but tactile in origin. This piece makes that correspondence visible, rendering the algebraic structure of woven patterns as visual compositions. Classic drafts — point twill, rosepath, huck lace, satin, plain weave — provide the threading, treadling, and tie-up matrices, cycling and combining to produce hybrid structures that are mathematically valid but may never have been woven by hand.

The extension to color is a tensor contraction: each cell holds an RGB triplet rather than a binary value, and the multiplication operates channel-wise across the three dimensions of color. The warp contributes one palette, the weft another, and the weave that emerges from their product is a third — determined entirely by the algebra. The loom becomes a computing device, and the fabric it produces becomes a visualization of multilinear algebra in three-dimensional color space.
