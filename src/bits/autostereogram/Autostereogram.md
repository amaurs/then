# Autostereogram

The Magic Eye books of the 1990s trained a generation to look _through_ images rather than _at_ them — to defocus, to surrender the eye's habitual grip on surfaces, and to discover depth hiding in apparent chaos. This piece extends that tradition into motion, generating animated autostereograms in which the hidden dimensional form shifts and breathes behind a veil of random pixels.

The technique demands active participation. The viewer must adopt an unfamiliar way of seeing — a parallel or cross-eyed gaze that the image itself cannot teach. In this sense, the work is incomplete without its audience. It exists as potential until the moment of perceptual convergence, when the flat grid of noise suddenly yields a volumetric form that was always there, waiting.
