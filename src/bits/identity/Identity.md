# Identity

Part of the color space collection. The identity mapping — the simplest possible function, which maps each input to itself — is here applied to the relationship between pixel coordinates and color values. Each pixel's color is determined directly by its position in the binary sequence of integers: the same bits that encode a location also encode a hue.

The result is the canonical ordering of digital color space, a kind of ground truth against which the other works in the collection can be measured. Where the other pieces employ sophisticated algorithms to rearrange the color cube into visually compelling patterns, this piece does nothing at all — and in doing nothing, reveals the inherent structure of the encoding itself. The patterns that emerge are not designed but discovered, artifacts of the binary representation that underlies all digital imagery.
