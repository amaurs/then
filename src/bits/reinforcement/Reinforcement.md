# Reinforcement Learning

A suite of animated visualizations built while studying Richard Sutton's foundational textbook on reinforcement learning. The piece renders the core algorithms of the field — value iteration, policy gradients, temporal difference learning — as spatial narratives: an agent, represented by a simple emoji, navigates a sparse environment toward a goal, its behavior evolving in real time as the underlying policy updates.

What began as a pedagogical exercise — a way to internalize the mathematics by making it visible — became a teaching tool in its own right, used to explain these algorithms to others. The animations make legible what equations obscure: the hesitation of an untrained agent, the gradual emergence of purposeful movement, the moment when exploration gives way to exploitation. The piece belongs to a tradition of mathematical visualization that treats understanding not as a precondition for seeing but as its consequence.
