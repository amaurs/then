# Colors

A collection of works exploring the totality of digital color space. Each piece in this series maps the complete RGB color cube — over sixteen million distinct colors — onto a two-dimensional canvas using a different algorithmic strategy. The collection functions as a comparative study: the same data, the same constraints, but radically different visual outcomes depending on the logic of traversal.

Taken together, these works reveal that color, in the digital domain, is not a continuous phenomenon but a discrete, enumerable set — and that the act of arranging this set is itself a form of composition.
