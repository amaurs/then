# Bolero

The bolero — that languorous, aching form of Latin American romantic song — is here subjected to a process of cumulative distortion. A corpus of bolero lyrics is fed into a discrete-time Markov chain, which generates new sentences based on the probabilistic distribution of words in the original texts. These synthetic utterances are then passed through machine translation, compounding error upon error as the signal degrades across systems.

The result is a kind of poetic entropy: love songs decomposed and reassembled by processes that understand probability but not longing. The Markov chain preserves the statistical skeleton of romance while discarding its meaning. The translation layer introduces its own distortions, its own misreadings. What remains are fragments that hover between sense and nonsense — ghost sentences that remember the shape of desire but have forgotten its name.
