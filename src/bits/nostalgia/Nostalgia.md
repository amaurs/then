# Nostalgia

A meditation on the aesthetics of degraded media. The piece processes imagery through a chain of post-processing effects — texture passes, dot screens, color matrix transformations — that simulate the artifacts of analog reproduction: the halftone patterns of newsprint, the color shifts of aged film, the grain of imperfect duplication.

The nostalgia in question is not for any particular image but for a mode of seeing — for the time when images arrived imperfect, when the medium's limitations were inseparable from the message's meaning. In an era of lossless digital reproduction, the deliberate reintroduction of noise and degradation becomes a form of resistance against the tyranny of fidelity.
