# Dense

A visual proof of a theorem in number theory: that the set of points (cos n, i sin n) for natural numbers n is dense in the unit circle of the complex plane. In other words, for any irrational angle, there exists a natural number whose corresponding point on the circle comes arbitrarily close.

The piece renders this convergence directly, plotting the sequence of natural-number points and watching them fill the circle with increasing density. What begins as a sparse scattering of dots gradually reveals the circle's continuous form emerging from discrete arithmetic — a visual demonstration that the integers, despite their gaps, can approximate any position on a continuous curve. The beauty lies in the inevitability: given enough time, the natural numbers will find their way everywhere.
