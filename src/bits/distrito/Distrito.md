# 1986

While working with satellite imagery between 2015 and 2018, the artist searched the Landsat archive for images of Mexico City — the city of his birth — captured in 1986, the year of his birth. Among the available passes, one stood out: a cloudless, perfectly clear exposure. It was taken on March 23rd. The exact day he was born.

The coincidence became an obsession. The piece explores the full combinatorial space of Landsat's multispectral channels, mapping each band — visible, near-infrared, thermal — onto the red, green, and blue channels of human vision. The result is a matrix of false-color composites of the same silhouette: Mexico City as it was known then, the Distrito Federal, rendered in every permutation of spectral truth. Grayscale images isolate individual bands; saturated monochromes reveal the city in pure red, blue, green; and the composite views produce landscapes of alien beauty — familiar geography made strange by wavelengths the eye was never meant to see.

The work is at once deeply personal and rigorously systematic: a birthday portrait taken from orbit, years before the artist thought to look for it.
