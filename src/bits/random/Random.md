# Random

Part of the color space collection. The baseline, the null hypothesis. All sixteen million RGB colors are enumerated and then randomly shuffled before being mapped sequentially onto the canvas. No algorithm organizes them; no structure governs their placement. The result is pure chromatic noise — a field of color that is, by every statistical measure, maximally disordered.

This piece exists to be compared against. It is the control group for the collection, the answer to the question: what does color space look like when no intelligence — human or algorithmic — intervenes in its arrangement? The answer is surprisingly uniform, a fine-grained static that the eye cannot resolve into pattern. Against this backdrop, the other works in the collection reveal just how much structure even the simplest algorithm can impose on the same raw material.
