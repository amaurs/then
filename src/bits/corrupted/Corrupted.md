# Corrupted

A glitch is a rupture in the contract between sender and receiver — a signal that arrives damaged, incomplete, or transformed in transit. This piece draws a direct analogy between digital corruption and political corruption: resources intended for the public good, intercepted and redirected by the few.

The visual language of the glitch — fragmented, displaced, eerily beautiful in its brokenness — becomes a metaphor for systems that function precisely by malfunctioning. The distortion is not an accident but a feature, a designed inefficiency that benefits those positioned to exploit it. The piece asks whether what we call corruption is simply the system operating as intended, with the glitch as its honest face.
