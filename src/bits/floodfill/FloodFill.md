# Flood Fill

Part of the color space collection. The flood fill algorithm — ordinarily a utilitarian tool for filling bounded regions in image editors — is here repurposed as a method of simultaneous traversal through two spaces: the two-dimensional image plane and the three-dimensional RGB color cube.

Beginning from a seed point in both spaces, the algorithm recursively explores neighboring positions, constructing two parallel sequences: one of pixels, one of colors. The resulting image maps the algorithm's exploration pattern directly onto the canvas, producing organic, branching forms that reflect the topology of adjacency in both spatial and chromatic dimensions. The flood fill's characteristic behavior — its tendency to spread outward from a center, filling connected regions before moving on — gives the image a quality of growth, as though the colors were propagating through the canvas like dye through water.
