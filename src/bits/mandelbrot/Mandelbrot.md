# Mandelbrot

The Mandelbrot set is an escape-time map of the complex plane: for each point _c_, iterate _z = z² + c_ from zero and record how quickly the sequence diverges. Points that never escape are colored black; points that do are colored according to their speed of departure. The boundary between these two behaviors — between the bounded and the unbounded — is where the set's infinite complexity resides.

Zooming into any region along this boundary reveals self-similar structures at every scale: spirals, seahorses, miniature copies of the whole, filaments of extraordinary intricacy. All of this from a single quadratic function. The piece renders this boundary as a heatmap, mapping iteration counts to a smooth color gradient that traces the fractal's edge with the precision of a topographic survey. Every pixel is the answer to a mathematical question; the image is the aggregate of millions of such answers, and it is beautiful.
